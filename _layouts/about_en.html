Alexei Munipov, Bolshoi Gorod

The topic of prison is increasingly in the centre of public attention in the context of arrests of opposition activists (their dynamics can be traced using the website ovdinfo.org) and other notable arrests.

Anton Oleinik, Vedomosti

Civil society responded to repression (still of the non-carnivorous kind) with an unprecedented response, at least without precedent in the past 12 years. [It responded] to searches and arrests with RosUznik, OVD-info and Agora

Yevgeniya Albats, The New Times

“They say that the law enforcement system is overloaded. We can see that. But what if it is the police who overloads it? They say that the prisons are overpopulated. What if the population is overimprisoned?” - these words from the manifesto of the Prisons Information Group, written by Michel Foucault in 1971 Paris, guided the founders of the Moscow project OVD-info

Anna Narinskaya, Citizen K

OVD-info, volunteer communities dedicated to helping the sick, the homeless, the drug addicts, all of this sprang up. Public life has increased in all its aspects, not just as protest activity.

Ekaterina Krongauz, Kommersant
